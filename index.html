Best Durga Puja Club of Varanasi

Akal Bodhan Samiti is a non-profit organization that has been a beloved part of the community for over 60 years.It is dedicated to organizing the annual Durga Puja celebration with all the rituals and practices that make it so special. For six decades, the Samiti has been a place where people come together to celebrate, connect, and honor the divine, fostering a strong sense of unity and belonging. Its commitment to keeping the spirit of Durga Puja alive has made it a cherished institution in the community, passing on these traditions to future generations.

Puja Itinerary

Durga Puja, a vibrant and grand festival celebrating the triumph of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura, unfolds over five days, beginning with Mahashasthi and culminating in Vijayadashami (Maha Dashami).This itinerary embodies devotion, celebration, and the timeless message of victory over evil. The itinerary typically includes:

Maha Shasthi

  • 28th September
  • Maha Shashthi marks the beginning of the main Durga Puja festivities, symbolizing the arrival of Goddess Durga on Earth. On this day, the Bodhan ritual is performed, invoking the Goddess and awakening her divine energy. It is a day of joy, as devotees welcomes Maa Durga and seek her blessings.

Maha Saptami

  • 29th September
  • Maha Saptami is the day when Goddess Maa Durga is worshipped in her full form, and it marks the beginning of her battle against evil forces. The day is highlighted by the 'Nabapatrika' ritual, symbolizing the Goddess's victory over negativity and the rejuvenation of nature.

Maha Ashtami

  • 30th September
  • Maha Ashtami is the most auspicious day of Durga Puja, dedicated to the worship of Maa Durga in her powerful form. It is marked by the Sandhi Puja, performed at the juncture of Ashtami and Navami representing the moment when Maa Durga defeated the buffalo demon Mahishasura.

Maha Navami

  • 1st October
    • Maha Navnami is the final day of Maa Durga's battle against evil, symbolizing the completion of her victory over the demon Mahishasura. It is a day of celebration , culminating in the 'Maha Aarti,' where devotees express their gratitude and seek the Goddess's blessings for prosperity and protection.

Vijaya Dashami

  • 2nd October
    • Vijayadashami signifies the victory of good over evil as Maa Durga returns to her celestial abode after defeating the demon Mahishasura.It is a day of farewell,marked by the immersion of Maa Durga idols in water,symbolizing the cycle of life, renewal, and the Goddess's promise to return next year.
